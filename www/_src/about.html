About HealthNear.me

HealthNear.Me is a simple tool for people to find public health providers near them.

There are a million ways to use the Internet to find a good restaurant or a place to get your oil changed nearby, but very few to locate much needed health services in your area, such as a mental health or WIC clinic. Existing tools are typically based on a single type of provider, don't work well on mobile devices, and are not searchable by location.

HealthNear.me aggregates ten types of public health providers in one place and makes them searchable, either by this website, which is built to work on smart phones, tablets, and old fashioned desktop computers, or via any phone that can send and receive text messages.

This application started as an entry in the Making Public Health Data Work Challenge. The creators, Chris Gansen and Melissa Buenger, are working with the Chicago Department of Public Health to pilot and deploy this tool in 2014.

Data

HealthNear.Me includes data from nine public datasets on the City of Chicago Data Portal and one set from the State of Illinois Data Portal:

  • Community Service Centers
  • Condom Distribution Sites
  • Cooling Centers
  • IDPH Hospital Directory
  • Licensed Substance Abuse Providers
  • Mental Health Clinics
  • Senior Centers
  • STI Specialty Clinic
  • Warming Centers
  • WIC Clinics
For the latest, most accurate information about public health providers, consult the datasets on the Chicago Data Portal. HealthNear.Me contains data accurate as of November 2013. HealthNear.Me makes no guarantee of the accuracy or veracity of this data.

Technical Details

HealthNear.Me is an open source project. We invite you to examine the source code on Github, and to contribute enhancements and bug fixes. Please don't hesitate to contact us with ideas for improvements or to report issues with the website.

We gratefully acknowledge the open source libraries and projects we used to build the site, including: the Go programming language, Jekyll, ElasticSearch, Kelly Dunn's go-geo toolkit, and the Elastigo project.
